Creating a network filter on VMware

over the summer a few years back, my brother needed a test environment for a network filter his work needed, but since most of my old computers were either nonfunctional or didn't have ethernet, i just made a few VMs on VMware and connected them together in a mock network environment where there'd be 1 normal computer and 1 network filter computer.

Creating an emulated PS/2 keyboard

a few months back I found an old dell server and decided to power it up so I could mess around on an old hard drive that only used ultra-ATA and to see if the card with that port still worked. The only issue is that I lost all my old ps/2 mice and keyboards and the only USB to PS/2 adapter I had was for mice only, so I decided to just create a fake one with an Arduino. I looked through some old documentations on the signals ps/2 keyboards put and out emulated that with the Arduino and finally got to the bios. I was able to figure out that the ultra-ATA card worked and I plugged in the hard drive, but it turned out it was completely encrypted, so it turned out the keyboard was useless anyways.

Bleeding the brakes on my car

a few weeks back at Walmart I spotted some dot 3 brake fluid for 6 dollars and figured "I should probably change my car's brake fluid since the last owner probably never did." So, I bought some and got some tubing from Lowes and got to work. I ended up spilling a bunch of ancient brake fluid on my old rims and on my newly fixed driveway when opening the bleeder valves on the calipers and I think some air got in the system, but at least I don't need to worry about having the pistons or master cylinder getting messed up any time soon (hopefully).
